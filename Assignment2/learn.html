What is a personal care home?

A personal care home is a residential home that is converted to accomodate individuals who are elderly, sick, or disabled.
It is a residence that provides shelter, meals, medical support, and supervision, for adults who need care. A personal care home,
offers individual attention in a residential facility. Personal care homes are smaller than nursing homes and typically offer it's
services to 2-20 residents.

Who is eligible to reside in a personal care home?

Persons who meet the criteria of becoming a resident of a personal care home can include:

  • Those who have the ability to live independently, with assistance,
  • People who need minor, (not full-time) medical assistance, from a qualified provider,
  • Individuals that show signs of needing assistance but are considered medically stable.

What is required to open a personal care home?

In addition to learning more about requirements needed on the American Caregiver Association website,
if you are a resident in Pennslyvania you would be required to:

  • In accordance with 55 Pa.Code § 2600.64(a), prior to working as an administrator in a Personal Care Home (PCH), administrators must complete the 100-hour administrator training course and pass the associated competency test. Administrators must also attend PCH Administrator Orientation.
  • Assisted Living Residence (ALR) administrators must complete the 100-hour course (a link is provided on it's main site), as well as a supplemental 15-hour course designed for ALRs. ALR administrators must also complete the ALR Administrator Orientation.
  • ​Administrators are required to complete annual training hours as indicated by their regulatory chapters. All trainings listed in this section are approved for PCH and ALR administrators.
